## Marketing & Funnel

**[March 2026] Do NOT pitch CCHC directly to freebie-only subscribers**
- Reasoning: These subscribers have never spent any money. Jumping to $89 is too aggressive.
- Decision: Pitch $5 calculators first. Move them to DMC/CCHC in follow-up sequences.
- Exception: Calculator owners (~1,525) ARE pitched DMC/CCHC directly — they've already purchased.

**[March 2026] Reactivation campaign uses 4 emails, not 3**
- Reasoning: Two distinct segments require different Email 3 pitches.
- Email 3A: Calculator owners → DMC ($59) or CCHC ($89)
- Email 3B: Freebie only → $5 Dosing Calculators

**[March 2026] HeyGen AI avatar approved for YouTube only**
- Reasoning: TikTok and Instagram Reels reward authenticity. AI avatar performs worse on those platforms.
- Decision: Use HeyGen for YouTube. Real Cheri (phone camera, casual) for TikTok/Reels when possible.

**[March 2026] TikTok and Instagram Reels are Month 2-3 priority**
- Reasoning: TikTok is brand new account. Instagram has been dormant 1+ year with no Reels history. Both need algorithm warm-up before expecting meaningful reach.
- Do not count on these platforms for near-term sales.

**[March 2026] Short-form content needs separate scripts from YouTube**
- Reasoning: YouTube is educational/structured (under 5 min). TikTok/Reels are entertainment-first with 2-second hooks and 60-90 sec max.
- Decision: Claude writes separate short-form scripts — never repurposed YouTube cuts.

**[March 2026] YouTube is top ongoing traffic priority**
- Reasoning: 30,000 existing subscribers. New video gets immediate warm views. Not starting from zero.
- Top performing content: potency, dosing, decarboxylation — double down on these topics.

**[March 2026] Course lessons stay behind paywall — YouTube teases only**
- Reasoning: Full lessons are the product. YouTube covers the WHAT and WHY, stops before the full how-to.
- CTA pattern: "Get the full lesson + [feature] inside [course name]"

**[March 2026] Email verification before sending reactivation campaign**
- Reasoning: Sending to bad emails hurts sender reputation and deliverability.
- Expected ~10% dropoff. Run verification first, then segment and send.
